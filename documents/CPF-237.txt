CPF-237 Sugarcane Variety
Origin and Development
CPF-237 is a high-yielding sugarcane variety developed by the Sugarcane Breeding Institute (SBI), Coimbatore, India. It was released for commercial cultivation in the early 2000s and has gained popularity for its excellent drought tolerance and high sugar content.
Morphological Characteristics

Plant height: 2.8 to 3.5 meters
Stalk diameter: Thick (28-35mm)
Stalk color: Green to purple
Growth habit: Erect with excellent tillering
Leaf characteristics: Medium width, good canopy
Bud prominence: Prominent oval buds
Internodal length: Medium (12-15 cm)

Sugar Content and Quality

Sucrose content: 16-18% (Very High)
Commercial Cane Sugar (CCS): 14-16%
Juice purity: 87-90%
Juice extraction: 68-72%
Sugar recovery: Excellent, premium quality
Juice Brix: 18-20%

Yield Performance

Cane yield: 90-120 tons per hectare
Sugar yield: 14-18 tons per hectare
Plant crop yield: 100-130 tons/ha
Ratoon crop yield: 85-115 tons/ha
Number of millable canes: 90,000-120,000 per hectare
Stalk weight: 1.2-1.5 kg average

Maturity and Harvesting

Maturity period: Early to medium (9-11 months)
Optimal harvest time: November to February
Early season sugar: High
Mid-season sugar: Very high
Late season sugar: Excellent
Ratooning ability: Excellent (4-5 productive ratoons)

Pest Resistance

Top borer: Moderately resistant
Stem borer: Good tolerance
White grubs: Moderate resistance
Termites: Good tolerance
Pyrilla: Moderately susceptible

Water Management

Irrigation requirement: Low to medium (highly drought tolerant)
Critical irrigation stages: Tillering and grand growth
Water stress tolerance: Excellent (key advantage)
Drought tolerance: Superior (survives extended dry periods)
Flood tolerance: Moderate

Nutrient Management
NPK Requirements:

Nitrogen: 180-220 kg/ha
Phosphorus: 70-90 kg/ha
Potassium: 120-160 kg/ha

Application schedule:

Basal: 40% N, 100% P, 40% K at planting
First top dressing: 30% N at 45 days
Second top dressing: 30% N, 60% K at 90 days

Micronutrients:

Zinc: 25-30 kg ZnSO4/ha
Iron: Ferrous sulfate if deficiency symptoms
Boron: 10 kg/ha for better tillering
Sulphur: 40-50 kg/ha recommended

Cultivation Practices
Planting:

Season: February-April (Spring), August-September (Autumn)
Seed rate: 32,000-45,000 three-budded setts per hectare
Row spacing: 75-90 cm (closer spacing beneficial)
Seed treatment: Hot water and fungicide treatment

Intercultural operations:

Gap filling: Essential within 21 days
Earthing up: 75-90 days after planting
Weed control: 2 herbicide applications or 3 manual weedings
Trash mulching: Highly beneficial for moisture conservation
Propping: Generally not required due to good standing ability
