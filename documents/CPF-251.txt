CPF-251 Sugarcane Variety
Origin and Development
CPF-251 is an early-maturing sugarcane variety developed by the Sugarcane Breeding Institute (SBI), Coimbatore, India. Released for commercial cultivation in the 2000s, this variety is particularly valued for its early maturity, good sugar content, and suitability for short-duration cropping systems.
Morphological Characteristics

Plant height: 2.6 to 3.0 meters (medium to tall)
Stalk diameter: Medium (24-30mm)
Stalk color: Green with purple tinge at nodes
Growth habit: Erect with good tillering
Leaf characteristics: Medium width, moderate canopy
Bud prominence: Prominent, oval shape
Internodal length: Medium to short (11-14 cm)
Leaf sheath: Light wax coating

Sugar Content and Quality

Sucrose content: 15-17%
Commercial Cane Sugar (CCS): 13-15%
Juice purity: 85-88%
Juice extraction: 65-69%
Sugar recovery: Good to high
Juice Brix: 17-19%
Early season sugar: Notably high (key advantage)

Yield Performance

Cane yield: 85-110 tons per hectare
Sugar yield: 12-15 tons per hectare
Plant crop yield: 95-120 tons/ha
Ratoon crop yield: 80-100 tons/ha
Number of millable canes: 80,000-105,000 per hectare
Stalk weight: 1.0-1.3 kg average

Maturity and Harvesting

Maturity period: Early (9-10 months) - Key advantage
Optimal harvest time: October to December
Early season sugar: High - Matures faster
Mid-season sugar: Very good
Late season sugar: Good
Ratooning ability: Good (3-4 ratoons)
Quick turnaround: Allows multiple cropping

Nutrient Management
NPK Requirements:

Nitrogen: 150-190 kg/ha
Phosphorus: 65-80 kg/ha
Potassium: 100-130 kg/ha

Application schedule (for 9-10 month crop):

Basal: 50% N, 100% P, 40% K at planting
First top dressing: 30% N at 30-40 days
Second top dressing: 20% N, 60% K at 75-90 days

Micronutrients:

Zinc: 20-25 kg ZnSO4/ha
Iron: Foliar spray if deficiency
Sulphur: 40 kg/ha
Boron: 10 kg/ha for better tillering

Note:
Responds very well to fertilizers, especially nitrogen in early stages
Cultivation Practices
Planting:

Season: February-March (Spring), July-August (Monsoon)
Seed rate: 32,000-42,000 three-budded setts per hectare
Row spacing: 75-90 cm (closer spacing beneficial)
Seed treatment: Hot water treatment + fungicide

Intercultural operations:

Gap filling: Essential within 25 days
Earthing up: 75-90 days after planting
Weed control: Pre-emergence herbicide + 1-2 manual weedings
Trash mulching: Beneficial
Timely operations crucial for early maturity
