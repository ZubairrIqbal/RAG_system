CPF-250 Sugarcane Variety
Origin and Development
CPF-250 is an advanced sugarcane variety developed by the Sugarcane Breeding Institute (SBI), Coimbatore, India. Released for commercial cultivation in the mid-2000s, this variety is known for its excellent disease resistance, stable performance, and adaptability to diverse agro-climatic conditions.
Morphological Characteristics

Plant height: 2.7 to 3.2 meters
Stalk diameter: Medium thick (26-32mm)
Stalk color: Green with purple flush
Growth habit: Erect with moderate tillering
Leaf characteristics: Medium broad leaves, dense canopy
Bud prominence: Moderately prominent
Internodal length: Medium (13-16 cm)
Leaf sheath: Moderate wax coating

Sugar Content and Quality

Sucrose content: 15-17%
Commercial Cane Sugar (CCS): 13-15%
Juice purity: 86-89%
Juice extraction: 66-70%
Sugar recovery: High and stable
Juice Brix: 17-19%
Reducing sugars: Low (good quality)

Yield Performance

Cane yield: 95-115 tons per hectare
Sugar yield: 13-16 tons per hectare
Plant crop yield: 105-125 tons/ha
Ratoon crop yield: 90-110 tons/ha
Number of millable canes: 85,000-110,000 per hectare
Stalk weight: 1.1-1.4 kg average
Consistent performance across seasons

Maturity and Harvesting

Maturity period: Medium (10-12 months)
Optimal harvest time: December to March
Early season sugar: Good
Mid-season sugar: Very good
Late season sugar: Excellent
Ratooning ability: Very good (3-4 productive ratoons)
Harvest window: Flexible, maintains quality

Disease Resistance Profile
Highly resistant to:

Red rot (Colletotrichum falcatum) - Excellent resistance
Smut (Ustilago scitaminea) - Outstanding resistance
Wilt disease - Strong resistance
Mosaic virus - Resistant
Leaf scald - Good resistance

Moderately resistant to:

Rust (Puccinia melanocephala)
Yellow leaf disease
Pokkah boeng
Ring spot

Minimal susceptibility:

Very low disease incidence overall
No major susceptible diseases reported

Top borer: Good tolerance

Termites: Moderate resistance

Climate and Soil Requirements
Climate:

Temperature: 20-36°C optimal range
Rainfall: 1500-2200mm annually
Humidity: 55-80% relative humidity
Photoperiod: Adaptable to medium day length
Frost tolerance: Moderate
Heat stress tolerance: Good
Climate adaptability: Wide range (tropical to subtropical)

Soil:

Type: Adaptable to various soil types
pH: 6.0-8.0 (tolerates slight alkalinity)
Texture: Sandy loam to heavy clay
Drainage: Prefers well-drained but adapts to medium drainage
Salinity tolerance: Moderate
Soil fertility: Medium to heavy soils preferred

Water Management

Irrigation requirement: Medium
Critical irrigation stages: Tillering, grand growth, formative phase
Water stress tolerance: Good
Drought tolerance: Moderate to good
Flood tolerance: Moderate
Water use efficiency: High
Responds well to both canal and well irrigation

Nutrient Management
NPK Requirements:

Nitrogen: 175-210 kg/ha
Phosphorus: 75-90 kg/ha
Potassium: 115-145 kg/ha

Application schedule:

Basal: 40% N, 100% P, 40% K at planting
First top dressing: 30% N at 30-45 days
Second top dressing: 30% N, 60% K at 90-120 days

Micronutrients:

Zinc: 25 kg ZnSO4/ha (basal)
Iron: Apply if deficiency symptoms
Sulphur: 45-50 kg/ha
Magnesium: 25 kg/ha in deficient soils
Boron: 10-12 kg/ha for better tillering

Organic inputs:

FYM: 25-30 tons/ha (recommended)
Green manuring: Beneficial
Biofertilizers: Azospirillum and PSB recommended

Cultivation Practices
Planting:

Season: February-March (Spring), July-August (Monsoon)
Seed rate: 30,000-40,000 three-budded setts per hectare
Row spacing: 90-120 cm (90 cm for better yields)
Seed treatment: Hot water (50°C for 2 hours) + fungicide

Intercultural operations:

Gap filling: Within 30 days of planting
Earthing up: 90-110 days after planting (important)
Weed control: Pre-emergence herbicide + 1-2 manual weedings
Trash mulching: Highly recommended
Inter-cropping: Compatible with short-duration crops initially
