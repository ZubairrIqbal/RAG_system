CPF-253 Sugarcane Variety
Origin and Development
CPF-253 is a premium sugarcane variety developed by the Sugarcane Breeding Institute (SBI), Coimbatore, India. Released for commercial cultivation in the 2010s, this variety represents a significant breakthrough in sugarcane breeding, combining very high sugar content with excellent disease resistance and adaptability. It is considered one of the most advanced varieties in the CPF series.
Morphological Characteristics

Plant height: 3.0 to 3.8 meters (tall)
Stalk diameter: Thick (30-38mm) - Premium girth
Stalk color: Dark green with purple nodes
Growth habit: Erect and vigorous with excellent tillering
Leaf characteristics: Broad leaves, thick canopy coverage
Bud prominence: Very prominent, well-defined
Internodal length: Medium to long (14-17 cm)
Leaf sheath: Heavy wax coating (attractive appearance)
Stalk strength: Excellent, minimal lodging

Sugar Content and Quality

Sucrose content: 16-18% (Very High - Among the highest)
Commercial Cane Sugar (CCS): 14.5-16.5%
Juice purity: 88-92% (Excellent)
Juice extraction: 70-74% (Superior)
Sugar recovery: Premium quality, excellent crystallization
Juice Brix: 19-22% (Outstanding)
Reducing sugars: Very low (premium quality indicator)
Fiber content: Optimal for crushing and co-generation

Yield Performance

Cane yield: 100-130 tons per hectare (High)
Sugar yield: 16-20 tons per hectare (Exceptional)
Plant crop yield: 115-140 tons/ha
Ratoon crop yield: 95-125 tons/ha
Number of millable canes: 95,000-125,000 per hectare
Stalk weight: 1.3-1.6 kg average (Heavy stalks)
Consistently high performance across environments

Nutrient Management
NPK Requirements:

Nitrogen: 200-250 kg/ha (higher for maximum yield)
Phosphorus: 80-100 kg/ha
Potassium: 140-180 kg/ha

Application schedule:

Basal: 30% N, 100% P, 30% K at planting
First top dressing: 35% N at 45 days
Second top dressing: 35% N, 35% K at 90 days
Third top dressing: 35% K at 120 days (for late maturity)

Micronutrients:

Zinc: 30 kg ZnSO4/ha (important)
Iron: Ferrous sulfate 25 kg/ha
Sulphur: 50-60 kg/ha (enhances sugar)
Magnesium: 30 kg/ha in deficient soils
Boron: 12-15 kg/ha for better tillering
Manganese: 10 kg/ha if needed

Organic inputs:

FYM: 30-40 tons/ha (highly beneficial)
Green manuring: Recommended
Biofertilizers: Azospirillum, PSB, and potassium mobilizers
Compost: 15-20 tons/ha
Press mud: 10-15 tons/ha (sugar factory waste)

Cultivation Practices
Planting:

Season: February-April (Spring), July-September (Monsoon)
Seed rate: 35,000-45,000 three-budded setts per hectare
Row spacing: 90-120 cm (100 cm optimal)
Seed treatment: Hot water (52°C for 30 min) + fungicide + insecticide

Intercultural operations:

Gap filling: Essential within 30 days
Earthing up: 90-120 days after planting (critical for stability)
Weed control: Pre-emergence herbicide + post-emergence + 1 manual weeding
Trash mulching: Highly beneficial for moisture and nutrients
Propping: Generally not needed due to excellent standing ability
Detrashing: Can be practiced for cleaner stalks
